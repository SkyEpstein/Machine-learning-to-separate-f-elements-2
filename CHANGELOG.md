## 2026-07-01 Separation recommender (inverse design)
- Added the held-out-extractant test (sep_recommend_holdout.py), the honest prospective validation: hold entire extractants out (molecule-grouped) and rank the novel ones by predicted separation. Pooled over the 3 pairs with >=8 distinct held-out extractants (32 cases): direction 0.594 and |sep| Spearman 0.096 overall, rising to direction 0.765 and Spearman 0.196 on the confident half. Uneven per pair (Am/Eu direction 0.80, confident-half 1.00; Er/Tb 0.44). Only 3 pairs have enough distinct extractants to run the test, itself a finding. Honest verdict: vetting a truly novel extractant is weak and only usable on the confident subset, mainly for direction not magnitude. A random split would have lied.
- Constitution: added the organizing purpose (vet new extractants and conditions before lab work) to the Mission, and principle 11 (splits mirror deployment: entire extractants held out and vetted as novel; a recommender is not trusted until it passes the held-out-extractant test, not merely row cross-validation).
- Added sep_recommend.py: given a target f-element pair, it grid-searches conditions per candidate extractant (bounded to observed ranges) and ranks candidates by predicted separation, producing two shortlists per pair, an EXPLORE list (uncertain but promising, predicted |sep| >= 1, ranked by the optimistic upper bound |sep| + uncertainty) and a CONFIDENT best-bets list (ranked by the lower bound |sep| - uncertainty), always showing the confidence. The candidate source is pluggable: the current extractants now, a molGen (LLM-generated) pool later. Spec in specs/2026-07-01-separation-recommender/.
- Honest validation: for Am(III)/Eu(III) (18 matched systems) direction accuracy is 0.72 but |sep| Spearman is 0.07, so the magnitude ranking is low-trust; Dy/Nd and Eu/Gd had too few matched systems to validate. Encouraging sanity signal: the confident Am/Eu best-bets recover soft sulfur-donor ligands (a dithioamide at rank 1), the correct real-world chemistry. It is a discovery/triage tool: trust the direction and the chemistry it surfaces, not the predicted separation magnitude. This matches the adversarially-verified finding that new-extractant separation magnitude is not predictable.

## 2026-06-30 Audit, corrections, and documentation
- Fixed the .gitignore conflict markers, and recomputed the leaky Track B most-confident-10 percent figure on the condition-key split (honest R2 0.874, RMSE 0.430, replacing the old random-row 0.940); propagated the honest number through the deck, workbook, README, and methods.
- Tested a direct separation-factor (delta-logD) regressor against differencing the logD model, both under molecule-grouped CV. The direct model loses badly (signed R2 -1.92 vs 0.188), because matched-condition metal pairs span only 59 extractants and the direct model cannot generalize a new extractant's selectivity; so separation is best obtained by differencing, and even that is modest (signed R2 0.19, direction correct 66 percent). Script sep_factor_model.py, results sep_factor_results.csv and sep_factor_by_pair.csv.
- Added a manuscript-ready master results table (docs/RESULTS_TABLE.md and master_results_table.csv) consolidating every headline with its metric, regime, caveat, and source, and a UCB recommended-experiment screen (ucb_screen.py) that ranks candidates by the upper 90 percent logD bound, maps them to the D zones, and emits the best experiment per extractant (ucb_recommended_experiments.csv).
- Added the dedicated f-element separation-factor evaluation (sep_factor_eval.py), the |delta logD| between two f-elements obtained by differencing the logD model, reported with a full metric suite (signed and magnitude R2, direction accuracy, Spearman, useful-separation F1) in two regimes. Known extractant: signed R2 0.356, direction 72.6 percent, magnitude R2 0.166, Spearman 0.60. New extractant: signed R2 0.188, direction 65.6 percent, magnitude R2 -0.199. The order (which f-element wins) is predicted well even for near-identical neighbors, the size of the gap is hard, and knowing the extractant roughly doubles signed R2 and clearly improves Spearman, with smaller gains in direction and no improvement in magnitude. Figure sep_factor_eval.png, per-pair table sep_factor_eval_by_pair.csv (covers 23 f-elements, 28 oxidation-state species, including actinides).
- Tested whether separation-factor prediction improves with confidence (sep_factor_confidence.py), and adversarially verified it (within-true-gap-bin control, random-ranking shrinkage benchmark, nested bootstrap). For a KNOWN extractant confidence genuinely helps and survives the confounds: at the same true gap size the most-confident pairs are more accurate (gap-balanced direction +0.116, mid-gap bins +0.20 to +0.24), direction accuracy rises 0.726 to 0.789 (most-confident-tenth minus full, paired bootstrap +0.067, 95 percent CI [+0.029, +0.103], p=0.0002), Spearman 0.60 to 0.78, and the confidence-filtered normalized error (RMSE/spread 0.64 at top 10 percent) beats a random-ranking benchmark (0.80). All three controls are reproducible in the committed sep_factor_confidence_verify.py. For a NEW extractant confidence is not net-useful: direction accuracy declines 0.656 to 0.586 and the RMSE drop is fully variance shrinkage (normalized error ~1.0, no better than random), because the confidence is too weakly calibrated on unseen ligands (rho 0.20 vs 0.39); a within-bin control still shows a small genuine signal (+0.05) too weak to overcome the smaller-gap penalty. Figure sep_factor_confidence.png.
- Ran the separation tests with Dr. Zhang's own model (sep_factor_zhang.py): his XGBoost on his ECFP-centric features, differenced for the separation factor, on the shared data (his set is the same data as ours, so this compares models, not datasets). His model predicts separation about as well as ours: known extractant signed R2 0.369 vs our 0.356, direction 0.715 vs 0.726, Spearman 0.604 vs 0.601 (a tie on known extractants); new extractant signed R2 0.227 vs 0.188, direction 0.672 vs 0.656, Spearman 0.507 vs 0.462 (his model marginally ahead on new extractants, from the ECFP fingerprint that our descriptor-only setup drops). Both hit the same wall at full coverage: the order is predicted moderately (direction 67 to 72 percent), the magnitude poorly (magnitude R2 about 0.12). Separation prediction is bounded by the data and the task, not by whose model is used; the confidence layer is our model-agnostic addition and lifts the confident-slice number for either base model (his known top-10 percent 0.369 to 0.470, ours 0.356 to 0.591). Figure sep_factor_zhang.png.
